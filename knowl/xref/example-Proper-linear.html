Example 2.4.9.

Find the general solution of
\begin{equation*} y'+3y=2xe^{-3x}. \end{equation*}
🔗
Solution.
Since the equation is in the proper form, we first find the integrating factor:
\begin{equation*} p(x) = 3, \text{ so }\mu(x) = e^{\int 3 \, dx}= e^{3x}. \end{equation*}
🔗
Multiplying both sides of the DE by \(\mu(x)\) gives
\begin{equation*} e^{3x}y'+3e^{3x}y=2x, \end{equation*}
🔗
which we can recognize as
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{d}{dx}\left(e^{3x}y\right)=2x. \end{equation*}
🔗
🔗
Integrating both sides with respect to \(x\text{,}\) we have:
\begin{align*} \int \dfrac{d}{dx}\left(e^{3x}y\right) \,dx\amp =\int 2x\,dx \\ e^{3x}y\amp = x^{2}+c\quad (c\in \mathbb{R}) \text{.} \end{align*}
🔗
Solving for \(y\text{,}\) we obtain the general solution of the DE
\begin{equation*} \boxed{y=e^{-3x}(x^2+c)\quad (c\in \mathbb{R})} \end{equation*}
🔗
on \((-\infty,\infty)\text{.}\)
